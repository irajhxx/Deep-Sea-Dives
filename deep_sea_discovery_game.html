● Diverse species: More than 60 species are known to exist, and they include cobras, mambas, and coral snakes

● Highly venomous: Sea snakes are highly venomous-some even more so than land snakes-but are generally non-aggressive and rarely bite humans

● Fully aquatic: Sea snakes are fully adapted to life in the water, with their paddle-like tails, and never go ashore

● Breathe air: Although they stay in water, they come up to breathe since they do not have gills and require oxygen from the air

● Hold breath: They can hold their breath for 30 minutes to several hours, depending on a species

● Salt glands: Some glands under the tongue excrete salt taken in by sea water to maintain balance

● Live birth: Most sea snakes deliver their live young in the ocean; some, like Laticauda, lay eggs ashore

● Smooth scales: Their smooth scales reduce water friction, making them efficient swimmers unlike rough-scaled land snakes

● Diet: Sea snakes feed on fish, eels, and crustaceans. Certain species are already specialised to hunt for particular prey

● Distribution: They are found in tropical Indian and Pacific waters, where coral reefs abound. They never occur in the Atlantic

Yellow-Bellied Sea Snake!

● Distribution: Across the eastern Pacific, from California through to Peru, even in Hawaii

● Appearance: Black upper body with a yellow belly, well colored into the surroundings of the home

● Habitat: These fish like warm water, usually found in open ocean and coastal areas

● Behaviour: Usually docile, non-aggressive species; it is observed to protect when threatened

● Diet: Feeds mainly on fish, those especially running near the surface

● Reproduction: Ovoviviparous - it gives birth to live young that swim away immediately
