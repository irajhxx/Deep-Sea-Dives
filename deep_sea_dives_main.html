Deep Sea Dives!

THE SEA


The sea is a wide, expansive domain that holds countless wonders and plays an essential role in human life. Voluminous coral reefs, different kinds of marine species, and deep-sea life thrive beneath its surface to create established ecosystems that thrive in all four corners of the globe. The sea provides food and resources for humans, is a means of transportation, and plays a vital role in sustaining weather patterns that are conducive to life on Earth. In addition, the ocean offers endless opportunities for exploration and scientific discovery.

THE POTENTIAL


From the tiny plankton to the gigantic whales, the enormous diversity of marine life provides insights into biology and climate change. Many organisms of the sea synthesise several unique compounds, which may result in new medicines and treatments. Moreover, aquaculture and marine conservation could be sustainably done to meet food security challenges and protect biodiversity. Understanding our oceans is the key to understanding and preserving our planet's future.

THE MYSTERIES


The sea is a mystery that still keeps on marvelling human beings. From the disappearing cases in the Bermuda Triangle to the depth of the Mariana Trench, the ocean protects secrets that science has yet to find out. Ancient shipwrecks like the Titanic lie on the ocean floor, preserving stories of the past. The mysteries of the ocean remind us that there is still much to be learned, hence creating curiosity and wonder over what lies beneath the surface, and truly puts into perspective how little we have discovered, and how much more there is to immerse ourselves in about the little blue planet we call Home.
